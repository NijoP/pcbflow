# Tracewright Handbook — Learn the Framework Step by Step

A guided path for electronics engineers. No programming background needed. Read it
in order the first time; after that, jump to whichever step you're on.

Each page follows the same shape so you always know what to expect:
**Purpose · What you need · What you get · What you do · What the AI does · What good
looks like · Common mistakes · Checklist · Next step.**

## Two ideas to hold onto

1. **You are the engineer; the AI is your assistant.** It does the repetitive and the
   checking; you make the decisions. It never orders a board on its own.
2. **Nothing moves forward until it's confirmed correct.** Every step has a
   checkpoint. This is the same discipline as a good design review — just enforced
   at every stage.

> Stuck on a word like *API*, *Node.js*, or *repository*? It's in the
> [glossary](glossary.md). You rarely need these terms to use Tracewright — but they're
> there when you want them.
